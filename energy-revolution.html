Energy revolution

in the light of climate change

by Piotr Podsiadly

Hi! I am Peter Podsiadły and I want to share with you what I learned about the Earth's future. This year I became interested in the topic of climate change. I read conflicting information and wanted to know whether or not the Earth is warming.

Equilibrium temperature

L (1-α) ( π R2 ) = ε σ T4 ( 4 π R2 )

This simplified energy equation keeps incoming and outgoing energies in balance.
Amount of energy absorbed from the Sun's rays depends on solar constant L, ability to reflect light and the area of Earth's shadow.
Outgoing energy is radiated by the Earth in each direction. It depends on two constants, surface temperature and area.
Without atmosphere Earth's temperature would be -18°C.

Greenhouse effect

CO2 bending: wavenumber 667 cm-1 (15 μm) band

Earth's IR spectrum at altitude 70 km

Fortunately Earth's atmosphere contains greenhouse gasses. This slow motion animation shows CO2 molecule dancing to the rhythm of Earth's infrared waves. Bending frequency matching frequency of infrared wave increases the chance that wave energy will be absorbed.
Water vapor, carbon dioxide, ozone and methane absorb and re-radiate heat back to Earth.
The gap between red and blue lines is why we have average surface temperature of 22°C.

CO2 concentration increase


CO2 movie
In April CO2 concentration in Earth's atmosphere exceeded 407 ppm and for the last 800 thousands years CO2 levels were between 200 and 300 ppm.
This recent rapid increase of CO2 concentration causes global warming. We know it's human made CO2 because we can measure isotopic composition of atmospheric CO2 and compare it to CO2 that comes from the burning of fossil fuels.

Global temperature rise

April 2016 anomaly vs 1951-1980 [°C]

Scientists gather data from land and oceans. On average temperature continues to rise. Global warming does not mean temperature increases everywhere and all the time. There are still some places and periods where it got cooler.
You can see that permafrosts in Alaska and Siberia are significantly warmer.

Amplifying feedbacks


Melting permafrost releases methane which is a greenhouse gas. First picture shows how scientists measured it. Melted ice no longer reflects light making Earth heat up even more. Rainforest drying and more frequent forest fires release additional CO2. Increased temperature increases amount of water vapor in the atmosphere. All these feedbacks amplify the greenhouse effect, which increase temperature, which cause more positive feedbacks.

Extreme scenarios

+16°C vs
Most likely not in this century, someday the Earth will warm so much, that it will never get cooler again. Runaway greenhouse effect happened on Venus where ocean evaporated and all that water vapor keeps the surface temperature above the boiling point, which prevents water condensation.
Even 16 degree warming would make most of our planet uninhabitable by humans. Wet bulb temperatures above 35°C can result in lethal hyperthermia as human body would not be able to remove excess heat.
Picture by NASA, Minghong

More likely

United Nations predicts 2 to 5 degree warming by the end of this century.

What 2°C means to us

  • US$43 trillion (43x Polish GDP)
  • Increased food prices
  • 20% more violence

IPCC estimated that 2 degrees global warming comes with the cost of 43 trillion dollars in impact damages. Some of it paid by our government, which gets money from me and you.
Every year population on Earth increases by the size of two Polands. At the same time Earth loses one Poland of land suitable for growing crops. You can imagine it as if on January first 2017 there were 3 times more people in our country and all the food had to be imported. Next year one of our neighbours gets population tripled and loose all their farmlands. With this trend we are going to reach a point, where there is just not enough food to feed all humans.

Effects of global warming


200 M climate refugees by 2050
With more frequent droughts, sea level rise and extreme weather events increasing number of people will be forced to leave their home region. They will come to other countries - in peace, hopefully as they have reasons to be angry at countries emitting greenhouse gasses.
Man kind is likely to survive, but other species are out of luck. Ocean acidification and other climate changes will disrupt food chains and extinct 1/3 of species.
The greatest threat to our planet is the belief that someone else will save it.

What has to change?

There is a single action that makes the most impact - leaving fossil fuels in the ground, where they belong to. If we do that, the Earth would still warm for 30 years, but then it would start going back to colder state.
Picture by tsbreview

We need energy


World energy supply
It's not that easy to stop using fossil fuels when 2/3 of electricity and 80% of total energy comes from them. They are very efficient source of energy. One liter of crude oil gives 10 kWh. Physically working human can daily produce 1 kWh, so burning 1 liter of oil is like hiring 10 people for one day.

Energy consumption by sector

Average Pole needs 82 kWh for transportation, heating and electricity. Since majority of energy comes from fossil fuels all of our daily actions pollute atmosphere with CO2 and contribute to global warming.

The end goal

0% energy from fossil fuels

We can and should reduce our energy needs per person and at the same time increase availability of renewable energy.

I found hundreds of respected scientific organizations endorsing the consensus that climate change is caused by human action. As I continued doing the research it was increasingly more difficult to find skeptics.

The consensus of evidence

Scientific debate was over in 2008. Vast majority of scientists agreed that human emissions of CO2 cause observed global warming.

Consensus on consensus

Multiple studies were made aggregating tens of thousands research papers. 97% consensus is the most consistent.
Unfortunately, discussions where almost everyone agrees are not interesting, so media typically take one person from the 97% and one from the 3%. This creates balanced arguments that keep viewers excited waiting to find out which side is going to win.

From 50% to 97%

Level of scientific agreement on human-caused global warming increases with the level of scientific expertise in climate science.
Right side is represented by peer-reviewed articles by climate experts with at least 20 climate-related publications. Everyone starts on the left side and today we are going to make one step to the right.
