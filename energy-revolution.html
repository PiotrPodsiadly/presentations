Why so serious?

How global warming works?
As the Sun's rays reach the Earth's surface some are absorbed and re-emitted as heat.

The greenhouse effect

Greenhouse gases like water vapor and carbon dioxide absorb and reradiate some of this heat. Increased amount of greenhouse gasses in the atmosphere traps more heat and warms the Earth.

Amplifying feedbacks


As Earth's temperature increases ice melts and is no longer trapping methane, which is a greenhouse gas. Melting ice turns areas that were reflecting light into areas that absorb Sun's energy. Rainforest drying and more frequent forest fires release additional carbon dioxide. Increased temperature increases amount of water vapor in the atmosphere. All these feedbacks amplify the greenhouse effect, which increase the temperature, which cause more positive feedbacks.

Extreme scenarios

+16°C vs
Most likely not in this century, someday the Earth will warm so much, that it will never get cooler again. Runaway greenhouse effect happened on Venus where ocean evaporated and all that water vapor keeps the surface temperature above the boiling point, which prevents water condensation.
Even 16 degree warming would make most of our planet uninhabitable by humans. Wet bulb temperatures above 35°C can result in lethal hyperthermia as human body would not be able to remove excess heat.

We need energy


World energy supply
It's not that easy to stop using fossil fuels when 2/3 of electricity and 80% of total energy comes from them. They are very efficient source of energy. One liter of crude oil gives 10 kWh. Physically working human can daily produce 1 kWh, so burning 1 liter of oil is like hiring 10 people for one day.

Energy consumption by sector


Average Pole daily needs 82 kWh for transportation, heating and electricity. 82 kWh every day is like hiring 82 people for your lifetime. From that point of view average Pole is a king with an army of servants. For many people that’s not enough.

Transportation

We can do more with less. We can be more efficient with energy we have. For example petrol engine uses only 1% of energy from burning fossil fuels to move the driver. Most of energy goes to heat the engine and some to move one ton of steel along with the driver. 99% of energy is wasted - there is an area for improvement.

We know how


We have the technology. First picture shows solar house from 1939, which used solar radiation as a heat source for the winter. The technology advanced and got cheaper. When building new home you can pay additional 10% to have energy-producing instead of energy-consuming house. Some designs produce 3 times more energy than they are using.

Peer-to-peer energy

Which introduces an interesting concept of distributed energy network. If I join a common energy network I can sell my excess heat or excess electricity to other peers. If I have spare money I can invest in electric or thermal battery, purchase resources when price is low and sell them when demand is high. Even energy consuming houses benefit from energy producing neighbors. Buying local electricity and heat avoids long distance transmission fees and energy loss.

Consensus on consensus

Multiple studies were made aggregating tens of thousands research papers. 97% consensus is the most consistent.
Unfortunately, discussions where almost everyone agrees are not interesting, so media typically take one person from the 97% and one from the 3%. This creates balanced arguments that keep viewers excited waiting to find out which side is going to win.

From 50% to 97%

Level of scientific agreement on human-caused global warming increases with the level of scientific expertise in climate science.
Right side is represented by peer-reviewed articles by climate experts with at least 20 climate-related publications. Everyone starts on the left side and today we are going to make one step to the right.

Equilibrium temperature

L (1-α) ( π R2 ) = ε σ T4 ( 4 π R2 )

This simplified energy equation keeps incoming and outgoing energies in balance.
Amount of energy absorbed from the Sun's rays depends on solar constant L, ability to reflect light and the area of Earth's shadow.
Outgoing energy is radiated by the Earth in each direction. It depends on two constants, surface temperature and area.
Without atmosphere Earth's temperature would be -18°C.

Greenhouse effect

CO2 bending: wavenumber 667 cm-1 (15 μm) band

Earth's IR spectrum at altitude 70 km

Fortunately Earth's atmosphere contains greenhouse gasses. This slow motion animation shows carbon dioxide molecule dancing to the rhythm of Earth's infrared waves. Bending frequency matching frequency of infrared wave increases the chance that wave energy will be absorbed.
Water vapor, carbon dioxide, ozone and methane absorb and re-radiate heat back to Earth.
The gap between red and blue lines is why we have average surface temperature of 22°C.

CO2 concentration increase


CO2 movie
In April carbon dioxide concentration in Earth's atmosphere exceeded 407 ppm and for the last 800 thousands years CO2 levels were between 200 and 300 ppm.
This recent rapid increase of CO2 concentration causes global warming. We know it's human made CO2 because we can measure isotopic composition of atmospheric CO2 and compare it to CO2 that comes from the burning of fossil fuels.
